Who we are

Meet the founders

Darion D'Anjou

Co-Founder

Darion D'Anjou is an award-winning filmmaker and generative AI artist who founded AI Makers Generation. He began his career in visual effects and science-fiction filmmaking at Digital Domain — the studio founded by James Cameron — where he contributed to films including Spider-Man: No Way Home, Black Panther: Wakanda Forever, and HBO's The Last of Us. Today he writes and directs AI short films that have earned recognition at festivals and online; his sci-fi short Pony swept Atlanta's Constellation Film Festival, taking seven awards including Best Picture, Direction, Cinematography, and Editing. A Wharton School graduate, Darion pairs a producer's discipline with deep fluency in tools like Midjourney, Runway, and ChatGPT. He started AIMG to give Atlanta creators a practical, hands-on path into AI — turning curiosity into finished, portfolio-ready work in eight weeks.

Gheri Thomas

Co-Founder

Gheri Thomas is a classically trained fashion illustrator and designer, and a co-founder of AI Makers Generation. She holds a B.F.A. in Fashion Design from the Savannah College of Art and Design, where she tutored peers in rendering and digital concept creation. Across more than a decade in practice she has spanned illustration, branding, and technical design — creating fashion flats for established brands and custom work for notable clients. Today Gheri blends that traditional foundation with generative AI image tools, merging human craft with machine-generated creativity to make emotionally resonant, distinctive work. Through her studio, the Gheri Thomas Collective, her philosophy is simple: creativity isn't just practiced, it's pushed to its edges. She co-founded AIMG to help other artists cross that same bridge — to treat AI not as a threat to their craft, but as a new brush to build with confidently.

Questions

The things people ask

Do I need any experience with AI or coding?

No. We teach every level, and most people arrive having only tried ChatGPT a few times. The cohort application asks where you're starting from — there are no wrong answers.

What does each thing cost?

Film Bar AI is free, every Tuesday. Parking is free too.

Workshop Wednesdays are free to attend. A $15 donation is suggested and entirely optional. Nobody is turned away.

The cohort is $800, paid in full when you apply.

Can I pay the cohort tuition in installments, or leave a deposit?

No. Tuition is $800 and must be paid in full — we don't take partial payments, and we don't hold seats with a deposit. You pay securely by card when you apply.

If that's a barrier, come to Film Bar AI on a Tuesday and talk to us in person.

Do I have to attend the workshops in order?

No. Every lesson stands alone. Join chronologically or sporadically — the curriculum compounds into a working portfolio either way.

What do I bring?

To Film Bar AI: yourself. A laptop if you feel like it.

To a Workshop Wednesday or a cohort session: a charged laptop.

Is there homework in the cohort?

Yes — every week, submitted at the start of each in-person session for peer critique and professional feedback. It's the part that works.

Will this get me a job?

We won't promise that, and you should be skeptical of anyone who does. The cohort prepares you: you leave with a résumé, a matching LinkedIn, a portfolio website, negotiation skills, a contract toolkit, and interview prep for AI industry careers. What you do with them is yours.

How do I join the community?

The WhatsApp group is where the conversation lives between events, and there's a Facebook group too. Both are linked in the footer. Members can also sign in to the community site for resources, events, and the AI Maker Bot.

What if I miss a cohort Saturday?

The application asks. Missing one is workable; missing two or more means you'd get more out of the Winter cohort. Tell us honestly and we'll talk it through.

We take tuition exactly one way: by card, at cohorts.aimakersgeneration.com. We never accept Zelle, Cash App, Venmo, wire transfers, or gift cards, and we never ask for money in a DM. If anyone contacts you asking to pay another way — or asking for a deposit to hold your seat — it is not us. Please report it to us immediately.
